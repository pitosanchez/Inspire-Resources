# Questions to Ask Your Transplant Team

## Why Asking Questions Matters

Asking questions helps you:
- Understand your transplant journey better
- Feel more confident and prepared
- Make informed decisions about your care
- Build trust with your care team
- Feel more in control of your health

**Remember:** There are no silly questions. Your transplant team wants you to understand everything. Don't be afraid to ask anything you're wondering about.

## Questions About Getting Started

### Starting the Process

- When should I start thinking about a transplant?
- How do I get started with the evaluation?
- Do I need a referral, or can I call directly?
- How long does the whole process take?
- What should I do first?

### Choosing a Transplant Center

- How do I choose the right transplant center for me?
- Can I apply to more than one center?
- What's your center's average wait time?
- What are your success rates?
- What makes your center different?

## Questions About Evaluation

### The Evaluation Process

- How long will my evaluation take?
- What tests will I need?
- How many appointments will I have?
- Do I need to do anything to prepare?
- Can someone come with me to appointments?

### What They're Looking For

- What are your requirements for transplant?
- What makes someone a good candidate?
- What might disqualify someone?
- What if I don't meet all requirements?
- Can I work on things to qualify later?

### Test Results

- When will I know the results?
- What do the tests show?
- Are there any concerns about my health?
- Do I need any follow-up tests?
- What do these results mean for me?

## Questions About Being on the Waitlist

### The Waiting Process

- How long will I wait for a kidney?
- What affects how long I wait?
- What should I do while I wait?
- How often will I hear from you?
- Should I stay on dialysis while I wait?

### Staying Active on the List

- Do I need to do anything to stay active on the list?
- How do I update my contact information?
- What if I move or change my phone number?
- Can I travel while I'm on the list?
- What happens if I get sick while waiting?

### Getting "The Call"

- How will you contact me when a kidney is available?
- What should I do when you call?
- How much time do I have to get to the hospital?
- What happens if I'm not available?
- What if I'm sick when you call?

## Questions About Surgery

### Before Surgery

- How long will the surgery take?
- What kind of surgery will I have?
- Will you remove my old kidneys?
- What are the risks of surgery?
- How should I prepare for surgery?

### During Surgery

- What will happen during the surgery?
- How long will I be in the operating room?
- Will I be awake or asleep?
- Can my family be there?
- Who will be doing the surgery?

### After Surgery

- How long will I stay in the hospital?
- What will recovery be like?
- How much pain should I expect?
- What happens right after surgery?
- When can I go home?

## Questions About Recovery

### Immediate Recovery

- What should I expect the first few days?
- How long before I feel better?
- What can I do, and what should I avoid?
- How do I take care of my incision?
- When can I return to normal activities?

### Long-Term Recovery

- How long until I'm fully recovered?
- When can I go back to work?
- When can I exercise?
- Are there activities I can't do anymore?
- What's normal, and what should I worry about?

### Warning Signs

- What problems should I watch for?
- What are signs of rejection?
- What are signs of infection?
- When should I call you?
- When should I go to the emergency room?

## Questions About Medications

### Anti-Rejection Medications

- What medications will I need to take?
- Why do I need to take them?
- How long will I need to take them?
- What do they do?
- Are there side effects?

### Taking Medications

- How do I take my medications correctly?
- What if I miss a dose?
- Can I take other medications with them?
- Should I avoid certain foods or drinks?
- How do I remember to take them?

### Managing Side Effects

- What side effects are common?
- What can I do about side effects?
- When should I call about side effects?
- Are side effects dangerous?
- Will side effects go away?

## Questions About Life After Transplant

### Daily Life

- Can I return to work?
- Can I exercise and be active?
- Are there things I can't do anymore?
- Can I travel?
- Can I have pets?

### Health and Wellness

- How do I stay healthy?
- What should I eat?
- Can I drink alcohol?
- Should I get vaccines?
- How do I prevent infections?

### Long-Term Care

- How often will I need check-ups?
- What happens at follow-up appointments?
- Will I always need to see you?
- What if I move away?
- How do I manage my health long-term?

## Questions About Complications

### Rejection

- What is rejection?
- How common is it?
- Can rejection be treated?
- How do I prevent rejection?
- What are the signs of rejection?

### Infections

- Am I more likely to get infections?
- How can I prevent infections?
- What infections should I worry about?
- When should I call about an infection?
- What happens if I get an infection?

### Other Problems

- What other problems might happen?
- How are complications treated?
- Can complications be prevented?
- What should I do if something goes wrong?
- Who should I call if I'm worried?

## Questions About Support

### Caregiver

- Do I need a caregiver?
- What does a caregiver need to do?
- What if I don't have someone to help?
- Can you help me find support?
- What resources are available?

### Emotional Support

- Is there someone I can talk to?
- Are there support groups?
- What if I feel overwhelmed?
- How do other patients cope?
- Who can help with emotional needs?

### Financial Support

- What will this cost?
- What does my insurance cover?
- Are there programs to help with costs?
- How much will medications cost?
- Who can help with financial questions?

## Questions About Living Donors

### Finding a Donor

- How do I find a living donor?
- Can you help me share my story?
- What should I tell potential donors?
- How do people get tested?
- What if I find a donor?

### Living Donation

- What are the benefits of a living donor?
- How is it different from deceased donor?
- What if my donor isn't a match?
- What is paired exchange?
- How long does it take with a living donor?

## Questions to Ask at Each Appointment

### Always Ask

- How is my kidney doing?
- Are my test results good?
- Do I need to change anything?
- Are there any concerns?
- When should I come back?

### Bring a List

- Write down your questions before appointments
- Bring someone with you to help remember
- Take notes during the visit
- Don't leave until all your questions are answered

## How to Ask Questions

### Tips for Good Communication

- **Be honest** - Tell your team what you're thinking and feeling
- **Be specific** - Ask exactly what you want to know
- **Take notes** - Write down answers so you remember
- **Bring someone** - A friend or family member can help ask questions
- **Follow up** - Call back if you think of more questions later

### It's Okay To

- Ask the same question more than once
- Ask for something to be explained differently
- Say you don't understand
- Ask for written information
- Take your time to think about answers

## Getting Answers

**If you're not getting clear answers:**
- Ask again in a different way
- Ask someone else on your team
- Request written information
- Bring someone with you to appointments
- Don't be afraid to speak up

## The Bottom Line

Your transplant team is there to help you. They want you to understand everything about your care. **No question is too small or too simple.** Ask anything you're wondering about - it's your right as a patient, and it will help you feel more confident and prepared for your transplant journey.

Remember: The only bad question is the one you don't ask!
