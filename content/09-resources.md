# Resources

## Helpful Organizations and Websites

There are many organizations ready to help you on your transplant journey. These resources can provide information, support, and assistance when you need it.

## National Organizations

### American Kidney Fund
- **What they do:** Help with financial assistance, education, and support
- **How they help:** Grants for insurance premiums, medication costs, and other expenses
- **Website:** www.kidneyfund.org
- **Phone:** 1-800-638-8299

### National Kidney Foundation
- **What they do:** Education, advocacy, and support for kidney patients
- **How they help:** Information, support groups, and resources
- **Website:** www.kidney.org
- **Phone:** 1-855-653-2273

### United Network for Organ Sharing (UNOS)
- **What they do:** Manage the national organ transplant waiting list
- **How they help:** Information about waitlists, transplant centers, and statistics
- **Website:** www.unos.org
- **Information line:** 1-888-894-6361

### Organ Procurement and Transplantation Network (OPTN)
- **What they do:** Maintain the national transplant system
- **How they help:** Transplant center directory, waitlist information
- **Website:** optn.transplant.hrsa.gov

## Transplant and Donation Resources

### Living Donor Assistance
- **What they do:** Help living donors with costs and support
- **How they help:** Financial assistance, information for donors
- **Website:** www.livingdonorassistance.org

### Transplant Living
- **What they do:** Education about living donation and transplant
- **How they help:** Information, resources, and support
- **Website:** www.transplantliving.org

### Explore Transplant
- **What they do:** Help patients understand transplant options
- **How they help:** Education, decision-making tools, resources
- **Website:** www.exploretransplant.org

### Living Donor Toolkit
- **What they do:** Resources for living donors
- **How they help:** Information and tools for people considering donation
- **Website:** www.livingdonortoolkit.com

## Financial Assistance Resources

### Patient Assistance Programs
Many organizations help with:
- Insurance premiums
- Medication costs
- Transportation to appointments
- Temporary housing if needed

**Ask your transplant center** about financial assistance programs they work with.

### Government Programs

**Medicare**
- Covers transplant and some medications
- Contact: 1-800-MEDICARE (1-800-633-4227)
- Website: www.medicare.gov

**Medicaid**
- State-based health insurance
- Contact your state Medicaid office
- Benefits vary by state

**Social Security Disability Insurance (SSDI)**
- Help if you can't work due to illness
- Contact: 1-800-772-1213
- Website: www.ssa.gov

## How to Use These Resources

### Getting Started
1. **Talk to your transplant center first** - They know what's available
2. **Ask your social worker** - They can connect you with resources
3. **Be specific about what you need** - It helps people help you
4. **Don't be afraid to ask** - Many programs exist to help

### Tips for Finding Help
- Start with your transplant center
- Be persistent - sometimes you need to try more than one place
- Ask for help - people want to help you
- Keep trying - resources are available

### Staying Organized
- Keep a folder of resource information
- Write down contact information
- Save websites you find helpful
- Keep track of programs you've applied to

## Questions to Ask About Resources

- What resources does your center have?
- What financial assistance programs do you work with?
- Are there support groups I can join?
- Who can help me with [specific need]?
- What programs help with medication costs?
- Are there resources for my caregiver?
- Where can I find [specific information]?

## Remember

**You don't have to do this alone.** There are many people and organizations ready to help you. Don't be afraid to ask for help - that's what these resources are for. Your transplant center is your best starting point for finding the resources you need.

**Help is available** - You just need to know where to look and ask for it. Start with your transplant center and let them guide you to the resources that can help you succeed on your transplant journey.
