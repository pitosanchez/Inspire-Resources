Inspire Kidney Transplant Resources aims to address these challenges by offering educational tools, patient navigation support, and community-driven interventions to ensure all individuals have a fair chance at transplantation.Structural racism, systemic barriers, and social determinants of health continue to limit equitable access to kidney transplants, disproportionately affecting Black and Brown communities. Many patients face hurdles at multiple stages of the transplant process, including referral and evaluation, due to biases in provider decision-making, food and housing insecurity, limited health literacy, and inadequate psychosocial support.

Our Community Health Worker (CHW) program is designed to screen for barriers, connect patients with clinical and community resources, and bridge gaps in healthcare access. From facilitating transplant referrals and scheduling medical clearances to providing assistance with transportation, medication access, and social support, CHWs play a vital role in advocating for patients. By integrating systemic solutions such as NLP-based provider bias identification, structured documentation, and targeted patient education, we strive to create a more inclusive and equitable transplant process.
