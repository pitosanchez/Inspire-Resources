Knowledge is Power

Understanding Kidney Health

Knowledge empowers you to take control of your health journey. Whether you're newly diagnosed or have been managing kidney disease for years, understanding your condition is the foundation for making informed decisions and living your best life.

💙

You Are Not Alone

Millions of people live full, active lives with kidney disease. With proper care, treatment, and support, you can too. Every day brings new advances in treatment and care.

🫘

What Are Your Kidneys?

Your kidneys are like your body's natural filters. They clean your blood, remove waste, and help control your blood pressure. Think of them as your body's water treatment plant - keeping everything clean and balanced.

The 5 Stages of Kidney Disease

Understanding where you are helps you know what to expect and how to prepare

1

Early Stage

eGFR: 90+

Your kidneys are working well, but there's some damage. This is the perfect time to take action! With lifestyle changes and proper care, you can often slow or stop progression.

✨ Focus on prevention
2

Mild Reduction

eGFR: 60-89

Slight decrease in kidney function. You might feel perfectly fine! Early detection is a gift - it gives you time to make changes that can protect your kidneys for years to come.

🛡️ Protect your kidneys
3

Moderate Reduction

eGFR: 30-59

Your kidneys are working at about half capacity. You might start noticing some symptoms. Many people live well at this stage for many years with proper management.

📋 Work with your care team
4

Severe Reduction

eGFR: 15-29

Your kidneys are working at about 25% capacity. This is when you'll start planning for the future. Modern treatments are amazing! Dialysis and transplants have helped millions live full lives.

🎯 Plan your next steps
5

Kidney Failure

eGFR: <15

Your kidneys need help to keep you healthy. This is not the end - it's a new beginning! Dialysis and transplants are life-saving treatments that work incredibly well.

🚀 Treatment options available

Common Types of Kidney Disease

Understanding your specific condition helps you get the right care

Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)

Most common type, often caused by diabetes or high blood pressure. Highly manageable with proper care!

Diabetic Kidney Disease

Kidney damage from diabetes. Great news: controlling your blood sugar can protect your kidneys!

Polycystic Kidney Disease (PKD)

Genetic condition with fluid-filled cysts. Research is advancing rapidly with new treatments being developed.

Glomerulonephritis

Inflammation of kidney filters. Many types are treatable with medications and lifestyle changes.

Lupus Nephritis

Kidney inflammation from lupus. Treatment has improved dramatically in recent years.

Acute Kidney Injury

Sudden kidney damage, often temporary. Most people recover fully with proper treatment.

Remember: Every Journey is Unique

Your kidney disease journey is yours alone, but you don't have to walk it alone. With the right information, care team, and support, you can live a full, meaningful life. Hope, treatment, and support are available every step of the way.

Your Complete Guide to Kidney Transplant

What is a Kidney Transplant?

A kidney transplant is a treatment option for kidney failure. It's surgery to give someone with kidney failure a healthy kidney from someone else (a donor).

Benefits:
  • Live longer than dialysis patients
  • Better quality of life
  • No more dialysis needed
  • Fewer food and drink restrictions

Your Transplant Options

Living Donor Transplant:

Kidney from someone alive - family, friend, or stranger. Usually lasts 15-20 years.

Deceased Donor Transplant:

Kidney from someone who has died. Usually lasts 10-15 years. Wait time: 3-5 years.

The Transplant Process

1
Talk to Your Doctor

Start the conversation early, ideally when eGFR is 30 or less

2
Get Referred

Your doctor refers you to a transplant center for evaluation

3
Evaluation

Complete tests to see if you're a good candidate

4
Wait for Surgery

Stay healthy while waiting for your kidney

Eligibility Requirements

Medical Requirements:
  • eGFR 20 or below OR on dialysis
  • Healthy heart and lungs
  • No active cancer or infections
Support Requirements:
  • Reliable transportation
  • Support person to help
  • Stable housing and phone
  • Insurance coverage

Finding a Living Donor

Living donor transplants have many benefits - shorter wait times, longer-lasting kidneys, and better outcomes.

How to Find a Donor:
  • Share your story with family and friends
  • Use social media to spread the word
  • Ask others to help spread the word
  • Leave brochures at gatherings

Caregiver Support

You'll need support throughout your transplant journey. Your caregiver can be anyone you choose - family, friend, or neighbor.

Caregiver Helps With:
  • Driving to appointments
  • Managing medicines
  • Household tasks
  • Emotional support

Questions to Ask Your Transplant Team

  • Am I a good candidate for transplant?
  • What are this center's eligibility requirements?
  • How long is the waitlist here?
  • Do you participate in paired exchange programs?
  • What support resources are available?

Myths vs Facts

Myth:

You must start dialysis before getting a transplant

Fact:

The best time is actually BEFORE dialysis (preemptive transplant)

Myth:

It's very hard to qualify for a kidney transplant

Fact:

Most people who apply are accepted onto the waitlist
