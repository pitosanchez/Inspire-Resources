About Us

Breaking Down Barriers to Care

At Inspire Kidney Transplant Resources, we recognize that many people, especially those from Black, Brown, and underserved communities, face serious obstacles when trying to access kidney care.

These barriers include racial bias in healthcare, financial struggles, lack of reliable transportation, food and housing insecurity, and limited access to medical information. This is not fair, and we are here to change that.

Knowledge is Power

What is Chronic Kidney Disease?

Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) is a condition where your kidneys gradually lose function over time. Understanding CKD is the first step in protecting your kidney health and making informed decisions about your care.

The 5 Stages of CKD

1

Stage 1

eGFR: 90+

Kidney damage with normal or high eGFR. Usually no symptoms.

2

Stage 2

eGFR: 60-89

Mild loss of kidney function. May have mild symptoms.

3

Stage 3

eGFR: 30-59

Moderate loss of kidney function. Symptoms may become more noticeable.

4

Stage 4

eGFR: 15-29

Severe loss of kidney function. Planning for treatment options begins.

5

Stage 5

eGFR: <15

Kidney failure. Dialysis or transplant needed to survive.

Understanding Kidney Health

Types of Kidney Disease

There are many different types of kidney disease. Each type has unique causes, symptoms, and treatments. Understanding your specific condition is an important step in managing your health.

Common Kidney Diseases

Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)

Long-term loss of kidney function, often caused by diabetes or high blood pressure.

Diabetic Nephropathy

Kidney damage caused by diabetes, affecting the kidney's filtering ability.

Polycystic Kidney Disease (PKD)

Genetic disorder causing fluid-filled cysts to grow in the kidneys.

Glomerulonephritis

Inflammation of the kidney's filtering units (glomeruli).

Kidney Stones

Hard deposits of minerals and salts that form inside the kidneys.

Urinary Tract Infections (UTI)

Infections that can affect the kidneys if left untreated.

Hypertensive Nephropathy

Kidney damage caused by chronic high blood pressure.

Acute Kidney Injury (AKI)

Sudden loss of kidney function, often reversible with treatment.

Rare Kidney Diseases

IgA Nephropathy

Immune system disorder causing IgA protein buildup in the kidneys.

Alport Syndrome

Genetic condition affecting kidney function, hearing, and vision.

Fabry Disease

Rare genetic disorder causing fat buildup in kidney cells.

Membranous Nephropathy

Immune disorder causing kidney membrane thickening.

Focal Segmental Glomerulosclerosis (FSGS)

Scarring of the kidney's filtering units.

Nephrotic Syndrome

Kidney disorder causing excess protein loss in urine.

Renal Artery Stenosis

Narrowing of arteries that carry blood to the kidneys.

Lupus Nephritis

Kidney inflammation caused by lupus autoimmune disease.

Minimal Change Disease

Kidney disorder causing protein leakage, common in children.

Goodpasture Syndrome

Rare autoimmune disease affecting kidneys and lungs.

Interstitial Nephritis

Inflammation of kidney tubules and surrounding structures.
